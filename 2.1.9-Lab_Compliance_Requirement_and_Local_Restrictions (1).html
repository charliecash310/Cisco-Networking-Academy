Complete  Do companies in your country need to follow compliance frameworks that are imposed by other countries? If so, what are the consequences for failing to meet the requirements of the frameworks and what are the penalties if there is a data breach?
Do companies in your country need to follow compliance frameworks that are imposed by other countries? If so, what are the consequences for failing to meet the requirements of the frameworks and what are the penalties if there is a data breach?
Answer Area
Answers may vary. If a company has customers who access their websites from other countries, that company must meet the local compliance requirements. Failure to do so, or losing data in a breach, can result in the company being banned from doing business in the country whose framework was violated. This is especially costly for companies that do business in the EU, since the GDPR has been adopted by all 27 of the EU countries. In addition, another 17 countries have GDPR-like compliance frameworks that are shared among groups of nations.
